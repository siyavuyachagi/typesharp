# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors, maintainers, and community members of TypeSharp pledge to make participation in this project a welcoming and respectful experience for everyone ‚Äî regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act in ways that contribute to an open, inclusive, and healthy community.

---

## Our Standards

### Expected Behaviour

- Be respectful and considerate in all communications
- Welcome contributors of all experience levels ‚Äî we were all beginners once
- Give and receive constructive feedback graciously
- Focus on what is best for the project and the community
- Show empathy toward other community members
- Credit others for their ideas and contributions
- Acknowledge mistakes, learn from them, and move on

### Unacceptable Behaviour

- Harassment, intimidation, or discrimination of any kind
- Offensive, derogatory, or insulting comments ‚Äî including those related to personal identity
- Publishing others' private information (e.g. email addresses) without explicit permission
- Trolling, deliberate provocation, or sustained disruptive behaviour
- Dismissing or belittling contributions based on experience level
- Any conduct that would be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all spaces associated with TypeSharp, including:

- GitHub Issues, Pull Requests, Discussions, and code reviews
- Commit messages and PR descriptions
- Any other channel where you represent or discuss the project

---

All reports will be handled with discretion and confidentiality. The maintainer is obligated to respect the privacy and safety of the person reporting.

### What Happens After a Report

1. The maintainer will acknowledge receipt of the report within a reasonable time
2. The situation will be reviewed and investigated fairly
3. A response will be provided explaining the outcome and any action taken
4. Where appropriate, the person who reported will be updated on the resolution

### Consequences

Behaviour that violates this Code of Conduct may result in:

- A private or public warning
- Temporary suspension from project spaces
- Permanent removal from the project community

The severity of the response will be proportional to the nature of the violation.
